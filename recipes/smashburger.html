Smash Burger

The Ultimate Smash Burger

Big beefy burgers are a thing of the past. There is nothing better than two thin all beef patties with lacey edges. Freshly ground from chuck and short rib, hand formed and smashed until they have a nice crust. Smothered with sauce, pickles and caramelized onions.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chuck
  • 1 lb boneless short rib
  • 1/2 lb american cheese
  • 1 onion
  • 2 tbs butter
  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • Martin's potato buns

Burger Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayo
  • 2 tbs ketchup
  • 1 tbs yellow mustard
  • 1 tbs pickle juice
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • pinch of cayenne

Toppings: pickles, lettuce, etc.

Steps

  1. Combine the burger sauce ingredients and mix well. Set in fridge.
  2. Cut the chuck and short rib into cubes and place in the freezer for 5-10 minutes. This allows the meat to grind more easily and not jam up.
  3. Place the grinder attachment on a kitchen aid mixer and grind meat through twice. See note below.
  4. Gently form the meat into 4 oz balls and place on a greased cookie sheet. Refrigerate.
  5. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil and butter in a heavy bottom pan over low heat. Thinly slice the onion and add to the pan as the butter starts to foam.
  6. Season with salt and a tiny bit of sugar and cook for about 30-40 minutes on the lowest heat possible.
  7. Preheat a cast iron pan over medium-high heat until starting to smoke.
  8. Add burger balls and press down using a spatula until completely flat, season with salt and pepper. (You can use a piece of parchment paper in between your spatula and the burger to prevent it from sticking)
  9. Cook for one minute then flip and top with cheese. Cook for another minute or until cheese has melted.
  10. Assemble the burger and enjoy!

Note: If you don't have a meat grinder you can simply mince the meat with a knife. Again make sure that the meat is cold!
