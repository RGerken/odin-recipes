Pastrami

Description

Brisket pastrami is brined for a week in curing salt before being smoked low and slow.

Ingredients

  • 1 brisket flat 5 lbs

For the Brine:

  • 1 gallon water
  • 1 cup kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup pickling spice
  • 5-6 cloves of garlic smashed
  • 2 tsp of Pink curing salt #1

Pastrami Seasoning

  • 1/2 cup ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup raw sugar
  • 2 tbs granulated garlic
  • 2 tbs ground coriander
  • 1 tbs dry mustard
  • 1 tbs granualted onion

Steps

  1. Combine 1/2 gallon of water, kosher salt, sugar and pink salt in a large stock pot over medium heat. Bring the misture to a slight boil and add the pickling spice and garlic. Once sugar is disolved, remove from heat and let cool.
  2. Trim excess fat from the brisket and place in a large container. Add in the brine mixture plus another 1/2 gallon of cold water.
  3. Refigerate for 5 days, flipping the brisket over each day. On the 6th day remove it and pat dry. Place the brisket on a raised rack and allow to air dry overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Prepare your smoker for indirect cooking at 275 degrees with hickory wood added to the hot coals for some flavor.
  5. Combine the seasoning rub and and coat the entire brisket well.
  6. Place the brisket on the pit and smoke for 5 hours or until the internal temp reaches around 165 degrees.
  7. Wrap the brisket in butcher paper and place back on the pit. Insert a meat probe into the center and monitor the internal temp until it reaches 202-204 degrees. This will take a total of about 10-12 hours.
  8. Rest in a dry coller lined with old towels for at least 2 hours before slicing.
